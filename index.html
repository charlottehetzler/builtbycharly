A note from me

Why I build the way I do

I started in business - studied it, worked in it, eventually did an MBA to go deeper. Somewhere along the way I discovered coding, and that changed everything. Seven years in, I still think the business background is my biggest advantage: I understand what needs to be built and why, not just how. The strategy, the user, the business case behind every feature request.

I've worked with startups and growing companies across the world - Berlin, Tel Aviv, Melbourne, Sydney. Every project is different, but my approach stays the same: build it with care, explain it clearly, and hand it over so you can run it without me.

That last part matters to me. I don't just deliver and disappear. I walk you through how everything works, make sure you're comfortable, and actively work toward the moment where you say "I've got it from here." That's not the end of a project - that's the goal of every project.

Beyond freelancing, I'm dedicated to giving back by mentoring women and aspiring developers. Empowering others to grow and succeed in tech is something I deeply care about — it's the same instinct that drives how I build: helping people gain confidence, then getting out of the way so they can move forward on their own.
